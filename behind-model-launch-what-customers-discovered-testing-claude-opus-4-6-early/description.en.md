## What is this post?
This post describes how a small set of customers test a new Claude model in a short pre-launch window, what they look for, and what they discovered while evaluating Claude Opus 4.6.

## When is it useful?
- When you’re planning an internal “early access” or pre-release evaluation of a new model.
- When you want a lightweight playbook for combining benchmarks with hands-on usage (“vibe checks”).

## Key points
- Teams clear calendars, create war rooms, and throw their hardest problems at the model first.
- Testing blends quantitative signals (benchmarks/evals) and qualitative signals (how it feels in real workflows).
- Reported approaches include automated evals + stress tests, SME qualitative evaluation (e.g., lawyers for legal tasks), iterative planning loops, and hands-on app building.
