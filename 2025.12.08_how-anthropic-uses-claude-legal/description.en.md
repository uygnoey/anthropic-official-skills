## What is this post?
This post explains how Anthropic’s legal team uses Claude to turn recurring legal work into repeatable workflows—especially marketing content review, contract redlining, outside business activity conflict checks, and privacy impact assessments (PIAs). It emphasizes building self-serve intake and triage so lawyers can focus on higher-judgment work.

## When is it useful?
- You support a fast-moving product/marketing org and need faster turnaround on reviews without lowering quality.
- You repeatedly review similar documents (NDAs, vendor agreements, marketing claims) and want consistent issue-spotting.
- You want AI-assisted intake/triage that keeps humans as final decision-makers.
- You want to package team “house guidance” into reusable files (“Skills”) and connect existing sources via MCP.

## Key points
- Start with pain points, not technology: identify repetitive, energy-draining work.
- Build self-serve workflows with human oversight (verify outputs; AI is a first pass, not final judgment).
- Use Skills (files Claude loads) for (1) workflow consistency and (2) voice/style consistency.
- Use MCP to connect Claude to relevant internal sources (e.g., Drive folders, Jira, Slack, Calendar) for richer context.
