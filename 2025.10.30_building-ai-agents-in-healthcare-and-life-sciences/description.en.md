## What is this post?
This post describes how AI agents are being deployed in healthcare and life sciences, focusing on real production examples and the constraints that make the domain uniquely hard (fragmented data, regulation, and safety).

## When is it useful?
Use it when you’re evaluating whether an agent is appropriate for a healthcare workflow, and when you need a practical checklist of design decisions around data integration, latency, compliance, and human clinical oversight.

## Key points
- Real-world outcomes: Pfizer reports saving 16,000 hours of annual research time, and Novo Nordisk reports reducing clinical study report generation from weeks to minutes.
- Three interoperability decisions: connectivity approach, data formatting, and synchronization requirements.
- Compliance and safety requirements: audit trails, governance, observability, validation, and fail-safe defaults with clear escalation.
